What is ASL?

American Sign Language (ASL) is a complete, natural language that is primarily used by the Deaf and hard-of-hearing community in the United States and parts of the world. ASL is a visual-gestural language with its own grammar and syntax, and it is not based on spoken English.

ASL relies on handshapes, movements, facial expressions, and body postures to convey meaning. It is a rich and expressive language that allows for complex communication.

As a virtual language, ASL has also gained popularity as a means of communication for individuals with certain language and communication disorders and for people seeking an alternative mode of communication.
